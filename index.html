Important Tips

  • During interviews, it is advisable to speak to your GitHub profile. While GitHub is not the end all be all of ways to measure candidates, some employers use it as a metric to determine how active and how much experience a candidate has.
  • If you are coding outside of github you should bring it onto your github profile!
  • Have clean and thorough Readmes (see below)
  • It is recommended to add your final project on your pinned repo list. Considering forking, if it‚Äôs not your repo. Make sure final projects have a complete readme.
  • Star newer and your best repos so that they are coming up on the first page; consider getting rid of your older repositories that don‚Äôt add breadth or depth to showcase your skills
  • According to Rohan at Highline Beta ‚ÄúOne big tip I give Jr. Devs is to start contributing heavily to Open Source projects. The easiest way to start is to start doing Pull Requests for grammar, move to documentation, and then start doing bug fixes. At the very least it shows me that they are willing to put in work while also learning new skills, and it's quick hacks to doing some real implementation work outside of a team.‚Äù

Interview Prep
v

Research the Company and Product
  • If they have a product, download/view the product and/or research it
  • Know the company website really well
  • Review other job descriptions
  • Check out the company on Glassdoor
  • Research the team: Find their members on LinkedIn
  • Know Why You're Intererested in working there: Take the time to think about why you are aligned
Logistics Prep
  • Know where you are going, but don't arrive more than 10 minutes before
  • Everyone is watching, not just the person interviewing you. Always be polite
Prep for the Interview
  • Review the techstack: If you aren't familiar with it, learn some basics so you can show your eagerness to learn and growth mindset
  • Know the job description: If there is no job description, find other positions in the same company
  • Know thyself: know how you align with this company and job
  • Check out the culture fit and tech interview sections for practice questions to run through
Answers You Should Have Prepared
  • Tell me about yourself
  • Scenerio Questions: A time you had a conflict working with others, A time you failed
  • Have 3 strengths and weaknesses in your back pocket
  • Have 5-7 questions ready that you want to ask the employer: Check out Culture Fit Interview Section for questions to bring

Interview Follow-Up
v

Follow-up is key!

When Do I Follow-Up?
  • You should always send a follow up email following your interview (a few hours later or the day after)
  • Pro Tip: Your last question in an interview should always be about next steps. If you do not hear by when they let you know they will get back to you, you can also send a follow-up email the day after you expected to hear.

Interview Follow-up Sample email:

I hope this note finds you well. Thanks again for taking the time to meet with me, and those additional tips on how to approach my final project. I took some time to dig deeper into X, and learned more about Y as well. You mentioned that you‚Äôd be making a decision for the ‚Äúposition title‚Äù role by the end of last week. Do you have any updates on the role? I‚Äôm still very interested in joining ‚ÄúCompany Name‚Äù.

Please let me know if there‚Äôs anything I can provide to assist you in your decision-making process.
Thank you,
Tom
